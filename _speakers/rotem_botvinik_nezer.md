ShortBio: Rotem Botvinik-Nezer is an Assistant Professor at the Hebrew University of
  Jerusalem and PI of the Reproducible Belief Neuroscience Lab. Her research combines
  cognitive psychology, computational modeling, and neuroimaging to study how beliefs
  and expectations are formed, updated, and how they shape mental, physiological, and
  social processes, with a focus on placebo effects. She has led and contributed to major
  open-science and reproducibility initiatives in neuroimaging and beyond, including work
  on analytical variability and on practical tools for open and reproducible research.
